Chapter Three
What the Blood Requires

The altar stood where it had always stood — at the edge of the eastern field, where the ground dipped toward the river and the morning fog gathered in slow, unmoving pools. Corvin had built it himself, stone by deliberate stone, before the eldest was born.

He did not look at it directly. He had learned, in the years since the first refusal, that looking at it directly made the wanting worse.

· · ·

Markesh found him there before the sun had cleared the ridge. The boy moved quietly for his age — a gift, or perhaps a habit learned from watching his father stand in silence at the edge of things.

"The lamb is ready," Markesh said.
